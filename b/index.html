Things people ask before they start

Six honest answers.

Does my recording get uploaded to a server?
No. All processing happens in your browser. Nothing leaves your device unless you explicitly export and share a PDF, CSV, or audio file. You can verify this in your browser's network panel — open DevTools, record, and you'll see zero outbound audio traffic.
How accurate is the dB reading, really?
Consumer microphones aren't lab-grade. Our readings are accurate to within ±3 dB on most laptop and phone mics, which is honest enough for civilian documentation and dispute resolution. For legal-grade measurement, you'll still want a certified Type 2 sound level meter. We publish the full methodology on our accuracy page.
Will a property manager, landlord, or court actually accept this?
Property managers routinely accept timestamped dB records as supporting evidence, especially when bundled with a short audio clip. Courts vary by jurisdiction; our Pro notarized PDF includes a SHA-256 hash and a third-party timestamp anchor that several small-claims courts have accepted. We're honest about what we are: a documentation aid, not a legal instrument.
Why is Pro $4.99 and not free?
$4.99/mo covers the notarization service, multi-device sync, and the 24-hour evidence review queue. The free tier stays free because noise is universal and so is documentation. We don't sell your data, run ads, or train models on your recordings — we can't, because the recordings never reach us.
Do you support languages other than English?
Yes — the full site and recorder are translated into 9 languages: English, 中文, Español, Français, Deutsch, 日本語, 한국어, Tiếng Việt, ไทย. Switch via the language pill in the top-right.
What happens to my data if I switch phones?
On the free tier, your records live in your browser's local storage. If you clear browser data, they're gone — export PDFs first. On Pro, records sync across devices with a 5-year retention guarantee, and you can pull a full export at any time from your account page.
Disclaimer: This tool is for civilian reference and field documentation only, not professional metering equipment. All audio and data are processed locally on your device unless you choose to export or share files.
